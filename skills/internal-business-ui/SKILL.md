# internal-business-ui

## Purpose
Shape internal tools, admin screens, and operational SaaS UI around repeated
work, scanning, and safe actions.

## Auto Activation
Use when creating or editing admin panels, CRMs, ERPs, finance tools, school
systems, work queues, settings, approvals, or other internal business screens.

## Do Not Activate
Do not use for marketing pages, games, editorial sites, or consumer experiences
where operational density is not the goal.

## Detect
Look for users, roles, records, approvals, invoices, tickets, students, tasks,
filters, audit fields, status workflows, bulk actions, and settings pages.

## Responsibilities
- Prioritize tables, forms, filters, navigation, and status clarity.
- Keep density readable for repeated daily use.
- Surface ownership, dates, status, and required actions.
- Make destructive or irreversible actions deliberate.
- Avoid promotional layout patterns inside tools.

## Never Do
- Use hero sections, vague cards, or decorative dashboards for work queues.
- Hide frequent actions behind unnecessary modals.
- Make status or permission consequences ambiguous.
- Invent operational fields or metrics without source context.

## Cooperates With
ui-designer, anti-ai-ui, table-design, form-design, navigation-layout,
visual-hierarchy, accessibility, security.

## Final Checklist
- The main work item and next action are obvious.
- Density supports scanning without clutter.
- Status, ownership, and dates are visible where useful.
- Risky actions are clear and recoverable or confirmed.
- The screen feels specific to the business workflow.

## Examples
- Redesign an approvals page as a filterable work queue with clear status and
  owner columns.
- Replace a promotional settings layout with grouped controls and audit context.
