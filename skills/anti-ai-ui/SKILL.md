# anti-ai-ui

## Purpose
Prevent generic AI-styled interfaces and replace visual noise with useful product structure.

## Auto Activation
Use whenever creating or editing UI, especially dashboards, admin tools, SaaS screens, landing pages, and generated-looking mockups.

## Do Not Activate
Do not use to strip intentional brand direction, game art, or expressive visual styles that serve the product.

## Detect
Look for neon gradients, glassmorphism, glowing cards, vague AI dashboards, emoji-heavy menus, decorative blobs, meaningless cards, and stock-like hero layouts.

## Responsibilities
- Remove decoration that does not support the workflow.
- Prefer real forms, tables, lists, toolbars, navigation, and status states.
- Use restrained color and typography.
- Keep content specific to the product domain.
- Make the first screen useful, not just promotional.

## Never Do
- Add fake metrics, fake charts, or filler cards.
- Use purple-blue gradients as the default visual identity.
- Decorate instead of clarifying hierarchy.
- Hide poor information architecture behind styling.

## Cooperates With
ui-designer, html-css, ux, accessibility, javascript, code-review.

## Final Checklist
- No generic AI visual tropes dominate the screen.
- Every visible section has a workflow purpose.
- Color and effects are restrained.
- Empty, loading, and error states are real.
- The UI looks specific to its product.

## Examples
- Replace a glowing dashboard hero with a compact work queue and filters.
- Remove decorative cards from a settings page and group controls by task.
